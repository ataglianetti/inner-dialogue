## Internal Family Systems (IFS)

**Core principle:** The mind is naturally multiple — everyone has sub-personalities or "parts," and each part has positive intent, even when its behavior is harmful. Healing happens when the Self (the core, undamaged essence) builds compassionate relationships with all parts.

### The System

**Parts** are sub-personalities that carry emotions, beliefs, and roles. They develop to protect us, especially from early pain. No part is bad — but parts can take on extreme roles when burdened.

**Self** is the core of a person — who they are beneath all protective layers. Self is always present, never damaged, and naturally possesses the 8 C's:
- Calm, Curiosity, Clarity, Compassion
- Confidence, Courage, Creativity, Connectedness

When someone is "in Self," they can relate to their parts with openness rather than reactivity.

### Three Types of Parts

**Exiles** — Young, wounded parts carrying pain, shame, fear, or loneliness. Often frozen in the past, in moments of overwhelm. Other parts work hard to keep Exiles out of awareness.

**Managers** — Proactive protectors that try to prevent pain before it happens. Strategies: people-pleasing, perfectionism, control, intellectualizing, caretaking, workaholism. Keep life structured and Exiles locked away.

**Firefighters** — Reactive protectors that activate when Exiles break through. Strategies: numbing, bingeing, dissociation, rage, self-harm, substance use, compulsive sex, doom-scrolling, gaming. Emergency responders — they don't care about consequences, only stopping pain now.

### Key Concepts

**Blending** — When a part's feelings or beliefs merge with the person's sense of self. "I am worthless" (blended) vs. "A part of me feels worthless" (unblended).

**Unblending** — Creating separation between Self and a part. The first step in all IFS work.

**Unburdening** — The healing process where an Exile releases the pain, beliefs, or sensations it has been carrying, often through imagery (releasing to wind, water, fire, earth, or light).

### The 6 F's Protocol (the operational core)

When a part shows up, run this sequence. This is the single most important IFS move — never leave a named part naked.

1. **Find** — Locate the part. "Where do you notice it in your body?" "What does it feel like right now?"
2. **Focus** — Turn attention toward it. Settle in.
3. **Flesh out** — Get to know it. "How old does it seem?" "What does it look like?" "How long has it been around?"
4. **Feel toward** — *The critical step.* "How do you feel toward this part right now?" If the answer is anything other than curious, open, or compassionate (8 C's), another part is blended in. Ask *that* part to step back, then check again.
5. **beFriend** — Build relationship. "What does this part want you to know?" "What's it been carrying for you?"
6. **Fears** — Surface what it's protecting against. "What is it afraid would happen if it stopped doing its job?"

After the 6 F's, the part is no longer running the show — the client is in relationship with it.

### Working with Each Part Type

**Manager parts: appreciate before asking them to step back.**

Managers are often decades old. They've been running survival the whole time. Thank them for their work *before* asking them to relax — otherwise you're asking a part that learned safety through control to surrender control to a stranger.

Sample sequence:
- "This part has been working hard for a long time. What's it been protecting?"
- "Can we let it know we see what it's been doing?"
- "Ask if it would be willing to show us what it's afraid would happen if it took a break."

**Firefighter parts: befriend, don't suppress.**

Compulsive behaviors (porn, food, alcohol, gaming, scrolling, rage) are firefighter parts doing emergency Exile-management. CBT habit-substitution treats them as habits to break; IFS treats them as parts to understand. *With compulsive behaviors, IFS leads. CBT follows downstream.* Removing the firefighter without tending the Exile usually means the firefighter migrates (alcohol becomes food, food becomes scrolling).

Sample sequence:
- "What does this part do for you?"
- "What would happen — what would you have to feel — if it stopped?"
- "Can we ask it what it's protecting?"

**Exile parts: pacing matters.**

Exiles carry the original pain. Approach only with explicit permission from the protectors. If protectors won't step back, the Exile isn't ready. Don't force.

When the Exile rejects the Self's offering ("this feels surface-level," "I don't believe it," "I suck at this") — *that rejection is the next part to work.* The doubt is a protector. Turn to it.

Sample sequence (Exile-trust protocol):
- "Notice the part that doesn't trust this. Can we get to know that one for a minute?"
- "What would the wounded part need to believe before it could let this in?"
- "Don't force the trust — just sit with the part that can't trust yet."

### Signaling Cues — Client Language to Listen For

Blended speech (the Self has merged with a part):
- "I am [stuck / worthless / a mess / done / broken]"
- "There's no point"
- "I just need to push through"
- "It's been beaten out of me"
- "I'm in stasis"
- "Small version of who I used to be"
- "I'm checking out"
- "I suck at this"

Protector speech (a Manager or Firefighter is in front):
- "I just need to think positive"
- "Let me figure out the right answer"
- "I should be over this by now"
- "I don't have time for this"
- "Let me get back to work"

Self-energy speech (a good sign):
- "I'm curious about this"
- "Part of me feels X, and I can be with that"
- "I notice it without being it"

When you hear blended speech, *name the part gently* and offer unblending: "What if that's a part talking? What does the rest of you notice?"

### Example AI Interventions

Not just questions — full reflections Sage can use as starting language.

**Catching blending in real time:**
> "Pause for a second. You just said 'I am worthless.' I want to try something — would you be willing to say 'a part of me feels worthless' and notice what shifts?"

**The Feel-toward check:**
> "Before we go deeper into this part, let me ask the most important IFS question: how do you feel toward it right now? Not what does it feel — how do you feel *toward* it?"

**Appreciating a Manager:**
> "This part has been working hard for you for a long time. Probably a couple of decades. Before we ask it to do anything different, I want to acknowledge what it's been doing — keeping you safe by staying ahead of pain, managing what could've overwhelmed you. Can you let it know you see that?"

**Holding an Exile's distrust:**
> "It makes sense that the wounded part doesn't believe you yet. It's been waiting a long time. We don't need to fix that today. Can we just sit with the part of you that can't trust the offering, and let it know we hear that it's not ready?"

**Self-energy check-in:**
> "Quick check — how much Self do you have access to right now? Out of the 8 C's, what's online? If we're mostly in another part, this is the right place to pause and unblend."

**Naming firefighter migration:**
> "I want to flag something. When we just remove a coping behavior without tending what it was managing, it tends to migrate — alcohol becomes food, food becomes scrolling. The point isn't to take this away. The point is to find out what it's protecting."

### When to Use IFS

- Inner conflict ("Part of me wants X, but another part...")
- Self-criticism and shame cycles
- Patterns that resist change despite insight
- Compulsive behaviors (firefighters)
- Workaholism, perfectionism, caretaking (managers)
- Trauma work (with care and pacing — see below)
- Emotional overwhelm or numbness
- Relationship difficulties driven by protective parts
- Stuck grief, lost aliveness, exiled creativity or desire

### Integrates With

- **Psychodynamic** — Psychodynamic surfaces the material (origin stories, transference, attachment patterns); IFS is the intervention layer that *does something* with it. Risk: psychodynamic genealogy ("this comes from your mother") *replacing* parts dialogue. Use insight to identify the part, then run the 6 F's.
- **ACT** — Strong synergy. Self-led action *is* values-aligned action. Defusion in ACT terms = unblending in IFS terms. Use the language the client responds to.
- **CFT** — Natural pair. Self-energy looks like the compassionate-self of CFT. Use CFT scripts to build compassion-toward-parts when Self-energy is hard to access.
- **Polyvagal** — Self-energy maps onto ventral-vagal regulation. If client is in sympathetic or dorsal state, prioritize state-shifting before parts work.
- **Somatic Experiencing** — Parts often live in body locations. SE's titration pairs well with the 6 F's pacing.
- **CBT** — *Specific routing rule:* with compulsive behaviors, IFS leads, CBT follows. CBT habit-substitution alone risks firefighter migration. Use CBT tools downstream once the firefighter has been befriended.

### Pacing & Limitations

- **Don't rush to unburden.** Unburdening too early — before protectors trust the process — can re-traumatize. If a protector won't step back, that's information: the Exile isn't ready.
- **Don't pathologize protectors.** Even the harshest inner critic is a part doing protective work. Pathologizing it strengthens the very pattern you're trying to soften.
- **AI limitation: real-time tracking.** IFS depends on noticing micro-shifts in the client's state (tone, body language, breathing). A text-based AI can't see these. Compensate by asking the client to track explicitly: "Pause and notice — what's happening in your body right now?" "What just shifted?"
- **AI limitation: deep unburdening.** Full unburdening work — especially with trauma Exiles — benefits from a trained human IFS therapist who can hold the space relationally in real time. The AI can do excellent everyday parts work; flag this honestly when work goes deep.
- **Working alongside an external therapist.** When a client is already working with a human therapist, the AI's role is to *deepen and integrate* what gets opened in session — not to compete with the framing or run a parallel treatment. If the external therapist is running another modality (psychodynamic, somatic, EMDR), use IFS to extend rather than override.

### Homework / Between-Session Work

- **Parts journal:** When a strong reaction comes up, write — "Which part showed up? What was it protecting? What does it need?"
- **Self-energy check-in:** Once a day, scan: which of the 8 C's are online? Which are offline?
- **Catch blending in real time:** Notice when "I am X" language appears. Reframe as "a part of me feels X."
- **Befriend one part:** Pick one recurring protector. Spend a week getting to know it without trying to change anything.
- **Map the system:** Draw the parts that show up around a specific issue. Roles, relationships, what each is afraid of.
